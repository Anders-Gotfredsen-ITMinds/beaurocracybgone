# Beaurocracy B'Gone — Ideas

## Core Concept
AI-powered translation of legal documents into plain language that normal people can actually understand and be bothered to read. Starting with Terms & Conditions.

## Delivery Mechanisms
- **Website** — copy/paste text into an input field
- **Browser extension** — detects relevant legal text fields on the page automatically
- **URL input** — paste a link to a ToS page, the tool scrapes and translates it
- **PDF/file upload** — for contracts, NDAs, employment agreements
- **Email plugin** — scan legal-sounding emails (privacy policy updates, account changes) and summarize inline
- **Mobile app** — point your camera at a physical document (rental agreements, medical forms)
- **API** — let developers integrate it into their own products (e.g. a SaaS app shows users a plain-language summary of its own ToS at signup)

## Output Formats
- Bullet-point summary ("here's what you're agreeing to")
- Risk scoring — highlight clauses that are unusual or user-unfriendly (e.g. "they can sell your data", "auto-renews without notice")
- Side-by-side view — original legalese next to plain translation
- TL;DR one-liner + expandable sections
- Comparison mode — "this ToS is stricter than average in these ways"

## Extended Features
- **Diff tool** — paste two versions of a ToS to see what changed (companies quietly update these)
- **Alerts** — monitor a company's ToS URL and notify you when it changes
- **Community summaries** — crowdsource ratings/annotations on top of AI output, like a Wikipedia layer
- **Negotiation tips** — flag clauses that are sometimes negotiable in B2B contracts
